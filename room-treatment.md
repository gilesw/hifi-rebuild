Based on the Darko video's principles and your specific setup, here are your Rental-Friendly options:
1. The "Back Wall" Diffuser: Optimizing your Bookshelf

Since your speakers fire directly at a floorstanding bookshelf, you already have a "Schroeder Diffuser" in the making. In a narrow room, the back wall is where bass builds up and high frequencies "slap" back at you.

    The "Randomized" Trick: Don't line your books up perfectly flush. Pull some forward and push some back to create an irregular surface. This breaks up high-frequency reflections (diffusion) rather than just bouncing them back like a flat wall.

    The "Bass Trap" Hack: Fill the bottom two shelves with the heaviest, densest books you have (encyclopedias, heavy hardbacks). This adds mass to the room's boundary.

    The "Soft Gap": If there's a gap between the top of the books and the shelf above, tuck some rolled-up acoustic foam or even thick towels behind the books. This turns the shelf into a secret "broadband absorber."

2. "Acoustic Art": Swapping vs. Hiding

Since you already have pictures hanging, you have "legal" holes in the wall.

    Option A (The Swap): Replace your current canvas prints with GIK Acoustic Art Panels (~£150). They look exactly like high-quality art but are 5cm–10cm thick professional absorbers. They use the same hooks your current pictures use.

    Option B (The Stealth): If your current pictures are canvas, you can buy 2" thick acoustic wool (like Rockwool or recycled PET) and tuck it inside the hollow frame of the canvas. It won't be as good as a pro panel, but it stops the canvas from acting like a "drum skin" and starts absorbing energy.

3. Freestanding Panels (The "Gobo" Strategy)

This is the most effective rental tip from the Darko video. You buy panels that sit on feet rather than hanging on walls.

    GIK FreeStand Panels (~£179): These are 1.5m tall. You can place them at the "First Reflection Points" (on the side walls between you and the speakers) while you're listening, and then just slide them behind the sofa or into a corner when you have guests.

    IKEA MITTZON (~£115): IKEA now sells freestanding acoustic screens. They are thinner than pro gear but very effective at cutting down "flutter echo" in narrow rooms.
